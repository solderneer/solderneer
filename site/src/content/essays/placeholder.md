## The Gap Between Vision and Timing

When General Magic set out to build the first smartphone in 1990, they had the right vision but the wrong timing. The technology wasn't ready. The market wasn't ready. The users weren't ready.

> Building something technically brilliant for users who don't yet exist is a special kind of disease.

## What We Can Learn

The lesson isn't to avoid ambitious projects. It's to understand that timing matters as much as technology. What users actually do matters more than what they should do in an ideal world.

Sometimes the best ideas are simply early. And being early, in technology, is often indistinguishable from being wrong.
